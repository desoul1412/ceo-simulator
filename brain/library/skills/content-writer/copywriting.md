# Copywriting Frameworks

## AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
1. **Attention** — Hook with a bold claim, stat, or question
2. **Interest** — Explain the problem and why it matters
3. **Desire** — Show the solution and paint the outcome
4. **Action** — Clear CTA with urgency

## PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution)
1. **Problem** — Name the pain point
2. **Agitation** — Amplify the consequences of not solving it
3. **Solution** — Present your product as the answer

## Landing Page Structure
- Hero: Headline + subheadline + CTA + social proof
- Problem section: What's broken
- Solution section: How you fix it
- Features → Benefits conversion (never just features)
- Testimonials / case studies
- Pricing (if applicable)
- FAQ (handle objections)
- Final CTA

## Rules
- Write for the reader, not the company
- One CTA per section (never compete)
- Every feature must map to a benefit
- Use specifics over vague claims ("saves 4 hours/week" not "saves time")
